Description

Neuro-endoscopic technical skills (NETS) training dataset is obtained from Neuro-endoscope box trainer, which is a box trainer used for imparting neuro-endoscopic skills. The activity performed on the trainer includes transferring 6 rings in a pre-defined manner using a biopsy forceps and endoscope. The activity of ring transfer is recorded by an auxiliary camera placed on the top of the trainer at 25 fps. T he whole activity is split into small videos or surgemes containing one ring transfer per video (approach, grasp, transfer and retract) and is used for evaluation. The videos were obtained from 6 neuro-endoscopic experts and 6 trainees with experience of > 10 and < 1 years respectively and were divided into 100 surgemes. All 100 short videos were given for blinded subjective evaluation by an expert neurosurgeon o n a scoring scale of 1-10 (1-least, 10-highest). 25 videos from this set are randomly selected as a test set and evaluated again at a later point in time to find intra-rater correlation.
